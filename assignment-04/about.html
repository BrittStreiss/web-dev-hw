About Robert W. Service

Robert William Service, "renowned poet of the Yukon" was born in
England, and wrote his first poem at the age of 6. He went to school
in Scotland, where he developed an interest in books and poetry
as well as a sharp wit and way with words.
He was curious and loved adventure stories, which
would eventually lead him to a life of travel.
At the age of 21, he fulfilled his dream of moving
to Western Canada and becoming a cowboy.
He wrote over 1000 poems, the most famous being "The Cremation of Sam McGee"
He served as an ambulance driver in WWI, where he met
and married a French woman. The two lived mostly in France
until his death in 1958.
